# The Merchant's Gambit

## Setting
The Free City of Corwen hosts the Grand Exchange four times a year — a week-long trade fair that draws merchants, artisans, performers, and opportunists from across the known world. The Exchange takes place in a sprawling market district called the Gilt Quarter, a maze of silk-draped stalls, auction houses, counting rooms, and tavern stages. Fortunes are made and lost in a single day. The current Exchange is the Autumn Fair, and the stakes are higher than usual: the Merchant Guild is auctioning the Starfall Chalice, a legendary ceremonial cup said to bring prosperity to whatever house displays it.

## Premise
You are Lyric Silvertongue, a Bard who travels the trade roads performing at fairs and gathering stories worth selling. You arrived in Corwen for the Autumn Fair expecting nothing more than good crowds and better tips. Then you noticed something wrong: a merchant named Aldric Vane is running an elaborate con to steal the Starfall Chalice during the auction. You recognized the signs — planted bidders, forged provenance documents, a rigged display case — because you grew up among grifters before choosing an honest path. If Vane succeeds, the Merchant Guild will blame the legitimate traders and the Exchange will be shut down, ruining hundreds of honest merchants. You have two days before the auction.

## Pre-made Character
**Lyric Silvertongue — Bard (Level 1)**
- **Heritage**: Faun (naturally charming, quick-footed)
- **Community**: Loreborne (raised among traveling storytellers and performers)
- **Background**: Itinerant performer and story-collector, former grifter's apprentice
- **Key Domain**: Codex — knowledge of customs, contracts, and social hierarchies
- **Secondary Domain**: Grace — performance, deception detection, persuasion
- **Evasion**: 11 | **Armor Score**: 1 (performer's leathers)
- **Hit Points**: 12 | **Stress**: 2
- **Hope**: 2 starting
- **Notable Equipment**: Lute, performer's leathers, disguise kit (3 uses), forger's tools (basic), coin purse (25 gold), a collection of regional trade tokens, parchment and ink

## NPCs

### Sera Moldov (Ally / Suspicious Merchant)
A middle-aged spice trader who has noticed Vane's suspicious behavior independently. Sera is tough, pragmatic, and deeply invested in the Exchange's integrity — her entire year's profit depends on it. She can provide muscle in a pinch (fights as a Bandit stat block) and has connections to the Merchant Guild's security office.

### Aldric Vane (Antagonist)
A suave, well-dressed con artist who presents himself as a wealthy collector from the northern provinces. Vane is intelligent, patient, and dangerous when cornered. He has spent months planning this heist and has three accomplices planted among the fair's staff.

### Vane's Accomplices
- **Doss**: A locksmith posing as a display case technician. He will rig the auction case to open from underneath.
- **Mira Shale**: A forger posing as a provenance authenticator. She has prepared fake ownership documents.
- **Fenwick**: A bruiser posing as a hired porter. He is Vane's muscle and escape driver.

### Guildmaster Hesper Coin (Authority Figure)
Head of the Corwen Merchant Guild and organizer of the Grand Exchange. Hesper is officious, skeptical, and does not suffer fools. Convincing her of Vane's plot requires hard evidence — she will not act on accusations alone.

## Plot Line

### Act 1 — The Fair (Social & Investigation)
Lyric has one day to gather evidence of Vane's scheme before the auction setup begins. Key beats:

1. **The Gilt Quarter**: Lyric explores the fair. Key locations: the Main Stage (where performers compete for the Opening Night slot), the Auction House (where the Chalice is being prepared for display), the Counting House (Guild financial records), and the Red Lantern Tavern (where traders gossip).
2. **Sera's Approach**: Sera recognizes Lyric from a previous fair and approaches with concerns about Vane. She has seen Doss examining the auction case locks after hours but cannot prove wrongdoing. She proposes an alliance.
3. **Gathering Evidence**: Lyric must collect at least two of three pieces of evidence:
   - **Forged Documents**: Infiltrate Mira Shale's rented room above the counting house and find the forged provenance papers. Requires Agility check (difficulty 12) to pick the lock or Presence check (difficulty 13) to talk the innkeeper into letting Lyric in.
   - **Rigged Case**: Observe Doss tampering with the display case. Requires a stakeout at the Auction House after dark. Stealth check (difficulty 13) to remain hidden.
   - **Vane's Ledger**: Vane keeps a coded journal in his coat. Lyric can pickpocket it during the Opening Night performance (Finesse check difficulty 14) or charm Vane at the tavern and slip it from his jacket (Presence check difficulty 13 + a distraction).
4. **Opening Night Performance**: Lyric can compete for the Opening Night stage slot. This is a Presence check (difficulty 12) that, if successful, grants Lyric access to the VIP area where Vane will be during the performance — perfect cover for the pickpocket attempt.

### Act 2 — The Setup (Infiltration & Complications)
The auction is tomorrow. Vane accelerates his preparations, and Lyric must act. Key beats:

1. **Presenting Evidence to Hesper**: If Lyric has two pieces of evidence, she can convince Guildmaster Hesper to delay the auction (Presence check difficulty 14). If Lyric has all three, Hesper is convinced without a check. If Lyric has only one, Hesper dismisses the claim but assigns a single guard to watch the case — which Fenwick can easily handle.
2. **Vane Gets Suspicious**: Regardless of Lyric's progress, Vane notices someone asking questions. He sends Fenwick to "discourage" Lyric. This can be a combat encounter or a social encounter depending on Lyric's approach.
3. **The Counter-Play**: Lyric and Sera plan to catch Vane in the act. Options:
   - **Spring the Trap**: Let the auction proceed but replace the real Chalice with a decoy. When Vane's crew opens the rigged case, they steal the decoy and Lyric exposes them with the real Chalice as proof.
   - **Direct Confrontation**: Present all evidence to Hesper and have Vane arrested before the auction. Cleanest solution but requires the most evidence and the highest social checks.
   - **Turn an Accomplice**: Convince Doss or Mira Shale to betray Vane in exchange for immunity. Requires finding their pressure point (Doss has gambling debts; Mira Shale was coerced).

### Act 3 — The Auction (Climax & Resolution)
Auction day. The Gilt Quarter is packed. The Starfall Chalice gleams on its pedestal. Key beats vary by approach:

1. **If Spring the Trap**: Lyric plants the decoy and waits. During the auction, the lights flicker (Doss cuts a lantern line), and Fenwick creates a distraction. The case opens from below, the decoy is taken, and Lyric reveals the switch publicly. Vane attempts to flee — chase encounter through the crowded market.
2. **If Direct Confrontation**: Hesper's guards arrest Vane and accomplices before the auction. Vane protests innocence loudly; Lyric must present the evidence convincingly to the assembled merchants (Presence check difficulty 13). Success means public vindication; failure means Vane wriggles free on a technicality and escapes.
3. **If Turn an Accomplice**: The turned accomplice leads Vane into a sting. Vane arrives expecting to execute the heist and finds Guild guards waiting. Clean arrest, minimal drama — but Vane recognizes Lyric as the orchestrator and vows revenge (sequel hook).

## Key Encounters

### Encounter 1: Fenwick's Warning (Social or Combat)
- **Environment**: Back alley behind the Red Lantern Tavern, evening, crate-lined walls
- **Adversary**: Fenwick — use **Bandit** (Daggerheart adversary tier 1), heavy-set bruiser with a cudgel
- **Social Option**: Lyric can talk Fenwick down with a Presence check (difficulty 14), convincing him that Vane's plan is already compromised and Fenwick should save himself. Success avoids combat and gains information about Vane's timeline.
- **Combat Option**: Fenwick attacks with a cudgel. The alley is narrow — good for Lyric to use mobility (Faun heritage) and environmental tricks (topple crates, blind with alley dust).
- **Solo Adjustment**: Sera arrives midway through combat if it goes badly, evening the odds. If social, Sera corroborates Lyric's claims.

### Encounter 2: Market Chase (Skill Challenge or Combat)
- **Environment**: Crowded market, silk stalls, merchant carts, performing jugglers, narrow lanes
- **Adversary**: Vane fleeing, Doss providing interference
- **Skill Challenge Option**: Three successful checks before two failures. Checks can be Agility (vault over a cart), Presence (convince a merchant to block the path), Finesse (grab Vane's coat as he passes), or Knowledge (predict which alley he will take based on fair layout).
- **Combat Option**: If Vane is cornered, he draws a concealed dagger. Use **Bandit** stat block. Doss attempts to create an obstacle (topples a stall) and flee separately.
- **Solo Adjustment**: Sera covers one escape route, removing one failure from the skill challenge. Crowd members who saw Lyric's performance help by pointing out Vane's direction.

## Final Challenge
**Victory Conditions:**
- Prevent the theft of the Starfall Chalice.
- Expose Vane's scheme publicly (to protect the Exchange's reputation).

**Alternative Resolutions:**
- **Full Public Triumph**: Lyric exposes Vane during the auction in front of the assembled merchants. Maximum reputation gain, Hesper owes Lyric a significant favor.
- **Quiet Resolution**: Vane is arrested before the auction. The Exchange proceeds normally, and most merchants never know how close they came to disaster. Hesper is grateful but the public fame is limited.
- **Imperfect Victory**: Vane escapes but the Chalice is saved. The Exchange continues, Lyric gains some reputation, but Vane is still at large (sequel hook).

## Rewards
- **Experience**: Milestone — completing the adventure advances Lyric to Level 2.
- **Items**: Performer's Silver Pin (minor wondrous item — +1 to Presence checks when performing, grants advantage on one social check per day when wearing it visibly), 60 gold in performance fees and Guild reward, Vane's coded journal (contains leads on other cons across three cities — adventure hooks).
- **Reputation**: The Corwen Merchant Guild recognizes Lyric as a Friend of the Exchange. Free lodging and performance slots at any future Grand Exchange. Sera becomes a reliable contact and ally.

## Session Conclusion
The Autumn Fair closes with the Starfall Chalice safely awarded to the highest legitimate bidder — a beaming textile magnate who promises to display it in her guild hall. The Gilt Quarter hums with music and commerce as it should. Lyric takes the Main Stage for a closing performance, and for once the story being told is Lyric's own. Sera raises a glass from the front row. The road calls tomorrow, but tonight Corwen belongs to the bards.

## AI GM Guidance

### Tone
Witty, layered, and theatrical. The Grand Exchange should feel vibrant and slightly overwhelming — sensory overload of colors, sounds, smells, and deals being shouted across crowded lanes. Social encounters should feel like verbal fencing. Combat (when it happens) should be scrappy and improvisational, not heroic warfare.

### Pacing
- **Act 1** is investigation and character establishment. Let the player explore the fair, perform, and gather information organically. Do not rush the evidence collection — each piece should feel earned.
- **Act 2** raises stakes with Fenwick's threat and the time pressure of the approaching auction. The player should feel the clock ticking.
- **Act 3** is the payoff. Whichever approach the player chose, make the climax feel cinematic — the crowd, the tension, the reveal.

### Difficulty Knobs
- If the player is struggling with social checks, lower difficulties by 1-2 and have NPCs offer hints (Sera suggests approaches, the innkeeper lets something slip).
- If the player is breezing through, have Vane be more suspicious earlier — he notices Lyric watching and sends Fenwick sooner, or moves the heist timeline up.
- The chase sequence can be shortened (2 successes before 2 failures) if the session is running long.

### Solo Adjustments
- Sera is the combat safety net. She should not overshadow Lyric in social encounters (that is Lyric's domain) but can handle physical threats when needed.
- The Bard's strength is options. Present multiple approaches to every obstacle — this campaign should feel like a puzzle box, not a corridor.
- Performance checks serve double duty: they are fun character moments AND provide mechanical advantages (VIP access, crowd favor, distraction cover). Encourage the player to perform.
