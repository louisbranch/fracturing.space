# The Lantern in the Dark

## Setting
The coastal fishing village of Brinewall clings to a rocky shore beneath a towering lighthouse known as the Ivory Beacon. For generations the Beacon has guided ships safely through the Shattered Straits — a maze of submerged rock formations that would tear any hull apart. Three nights ago the Beacon went dark, and two boats have already been lost on the rocks.

## Premise
You are Kael Ironshore, a Guardian who grew up in Brinewall before leaving to serve as a caravan guard on the inland roads. Word of the darkened lighthouse reached you on the road, and you have returned to find your home village gripped by fear. The village elder, Maren Tidecaller, suspects sabotage rather than mechanical failure — she has seen lantern oil diverted and strangers lurking near the harbor at night. She asks you to investigate the lighthouse and restore its light before the next trade fleet arrives in two days.

## Pre-made Character
**Kael Ironshore — Guardian (Level 1)**
- **Heritage**: Ribbet (amphibious heritage suits the coastal setting)
- **Community**: Orderborne (trained in disciplined guard service)
- **Background**: Former caravan guard, strong sense of duty to Brinewall
- **Key Domain**: Blade — Kael carries a weathered longsword and tower shield
- **Secondary Domain**: Valor — protective instincts, rallying presence
- **Evasion**: 9 | **Armor Score**: 3 (chain shirt + shield)
- **Hit Points**: 18 | **Stress**: 2
- **Hope**: 2 starting
- **Notable Equipment**: Longsword, tower shield, chain shirt, rope (50 ft), tinderbox, fishing hook set

## NPCs

### Pip (Companion / Guide)
A resourceful 12-year-old who grew up exploring every cave and tide pool around Brinewall. Pip knows secret paths through the sea caves beneath the lighthouse. In combat Pip does not fight directly but can use the Help action once per round, throw distracting rocks, or fetch items. Pip provides the social and exploratory balance a solo Guardian needs.

### Maren Tidecaller (Quest Giver)
The village elder and retired fisherwoman. Maren is tough, perceptive, and deeply worried. She can provide healing herbs (restore 1d6 HP, one use) and local knowledge.

### Voss "Blacktide" Corran (Antagonist)
A former merchant captain turned smuggler. Voss disabled the lighthouse so his crew could run contraband through the Straits without patrol ships spotting them. He commands a small crew of six thugs and operates from a hidden cove accessible through the sea caves.

### Lira Corran (Complication)
Voss's teenage daughter, held in the lighthouse keeper's quarters. She wants to escape her father's criminal life but fears retaliation. She can become an ally if treated with compassion.

## Plot Line

### Act 1 — The Village (Exploration & Social)
Kael arrives in Brinewall to find shuttered windows and nervous fishers. Key beats:

1. **Maren's Briefing**: The elder explains the timeline — the lighthouse keeper, old Dorin, has not been seen since the light went out. She suspects foul play.
2. **Village Investigation**: Kael can visit the tavern (The Salt Barrel), the dock, and the general store. Clues include: unfamiliar boot prints near the oil store, a torn piece of crimson sailcloth, and a dock hand who saw a lantern moving in the sea caves at midnight.
3. **Pip Joins**: Pip approaches Kael at the dock, offering to guide him through the caves in exchange for "a real adventure." Pip knows the cave entrance below the eastern cliff.

### Act 2 — The Sea Caves (Exploration & Combat)
The sea caves form a natural tunnel system connecting the hidden cove to the lighthouse basement. Key beats:

1. **Cave Entrance**: Tidal timing matters — the entrance is passable only at low tide. Pip knows the schedule.
2. **Trapped Passage**: The smugglers have set a crude net trap across a narrow ledge. A successful Instinct check (difficulty 13) spots it; failure means Kael is entangled for one round while two **Thugs** (use Daggerheart adversary: Bandit) attack from a side tunnel.
3. **The Hidden Cove**: A natural cavern with a beached longboat, crates of contraband (silks, unregistered weapons), and two more **Thugs** on watch. Combat encounter — the space is cramped, favoring Kael's shield-and-sword fighting style.
4. **Dorin's Cell**: The lighthouse keeper is alive but injured, locked in a storage alcove. He confirms Voss disabled the mechanism and has the ignition crystal.

### Act 3 — The Lighthouse (Climax & Resolution)
Kael ascends from the cave system into the lighthouse basement and works up through three floors to the lantern room. Key beats:

1. **Basement Workshop**: Sabotaged gears and smashed oil reservoirs. Kael can jury-rig the mechanism with a Finesse check (difficulty 11) if he recovers the ignition crystal.
2. **Keeper's Quarters (Floor 2)**: Lira is here under guard by one **Thug**. If Kael is diplomatic (Presence check, difficulty 12), Lira helps willingly and reveals her father's position.
3. **Lantern Room (Floor 3)**: Voss waits here with his lieutenant. He holds the ignition crystal and threatens to smash it if Kael advances. This is the climactic encounter.

## Key Encounters

### Encounter 1: Cave Ambush (2 Bandits)
- **Environment**: Narrow sea cave, ankle-deep water, dim light
- **Adversaries**: 2× Bandit (Daggerheart adversary tier 1)
- **Tactics**: One blocks the path, one flanks via a side passage. If Kael is entangled in the net trap, they attack with advantage for one round.
- **Solo Adjustment**: Pip throws a rock to distract one bandit (imposes disadvantage on its next attack).

### Encounter 2: Hidden Cove (2 Bandits)
- **Environment**: Large cavern, crate cover, beached boat
- **Adversaries**: 2× Bandit (Daggerheart adversary tier 1)
- **Tactics**: One takes cover behind crates (ranged crossbow), one charges. If Kael frees Dorin first, the old keeper can block the cave exit to prevent escape.
- **Solo Adjustment**: If Kael is at half HP or below, Dorin throws a crate lid as improvised cover (+1 armor for one round).

### Encounter 3: Lantern Room Showdown (Voss + Lieutenant)
- **Environment**: Circular room, glass lantern housing, iron railing, 80-foot drop
- **Adversaries**: Voss — use **Bandit Captain** (Daggerheart adversary tier 2); Lieutenant — use **Bandit** (tier 1)
- **Tactics**: Voss opens with intimidation, offering Kael a bribe to walk away. If combat begins, the lieutenant engages Kael while Voss attempts to flee down the stairs. Voss will not destroy the crystal unless cornered with no escape — it is his leverage.
- **Solo Adjustment**: If Lira is allied, she locks the stairwell door from below, preventing Voss's escape and turning the fight into a contained 2v1 (with Pip's Help action).

## Final Challenge
**Victory Conditions:**
- Recover the ignition crystal and relight the Ivory Beacon.
- Defeat or capture Voss.

**Alternative Resolutions:**
- **Negotiation**: Kael can offer Voss safe passage out of Brinewall in exchange for the crystal and Dorin's freedom. Maren disapproves but accepts if no villagers were harmed. Requires Presence check (difficulty 15).
- **Lira's Plea**: If Lira is allied, she can talk her father into surrendering. This avoids the final combat entirely but requires Kael to promise Lira safe harbor in Brinewall.

## Rewards
- **Experience**: Milestone — completing the adventure advances Kael to Level 2.
- **Items**: Ignition Crystal (minor wondrous item — produces bright light in a 30-foot radius, can ignite flammable objects on touch), 40 gold recovered from contraband, Voss's cutlass (fine steel longsword, +1 damage on critical).
- **Reputation**: Brinewall recognizes Kael as a local hero. Maren offers a permanent room at the Salt Barrel and priority passage on any trade ship.

## Session Conclusion
With the Ivory Beacon blazing again, the trade fleet navigates safely through the Shattered Straits. Brinewall celebrates with a bonfire feast on the beach. Kael stands at the lighthouse railing, watching the ships pass, knowing the coast is safe — for now. Pip is already planning the next adventure.

## AI GM Guidance

### Tone
Adventurous and grounded. Brinewall should feel lived-in — salt-worn wood, mending nets, stubborn people who solve problems themselves. The danger is real but human-scale. Voss is not a monster; he is a desperate man making bad choices.

### Pacing
- **Act 1** should last roughly 30-40 minutes of play. Let Kael explore and talk to villagers. Drop clues naturally — do not info-dump.
- **Act 2** is the action core. Keep cave descriptions atmospheric but brief. Two combat encounters with exploration between them.
- **Act 3** is the climax. Build tension on the lighthouse stairs. The lantern room confrontation should feel like a standoff before it becomes a fight (if it does).

### Difficulty Knobs
- If the player struggles in combat, have Pip's distractions be more effective (grant advantage on Kael's next attack instead of just disadvantage on the enemy).
- If combat is too easy, add a third bandit to Encounter 2 or give Voss a smoke bomb (creates obscured area for one round).
- The net trap in Act 2 can be skipped if the player is already low on HP.

### Solo Adjustments
- Pip is the key balancing mechanism. Use Pip for narrative color, puzzle hints, and occasional combat support — but never let Pip overshadow Kael.
- Dorin and Lira are optional allies who can reduce the final encounter difficulty if the player earned their trust through roleplay.
- Rest opportunities: Kael can take a short rest after freeing Dorin in the cave. Maren's healing herbs provide an extra recovery option.
