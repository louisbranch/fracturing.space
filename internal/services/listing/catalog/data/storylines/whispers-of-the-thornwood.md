# Whispers of the Thornwood

## Setting
The Thornwood is an ancient forest that stretches across the heart of the Verdant March — a region where druids, rangers, and wandering herbalists live alongside the wild. At its center stands the Hearthstone, a massive moss-covered menhir that pulses with faint green light. For centuries the Hearthstone has kept the forest in balance, regulating growth cycles and warding off blight. One week ago the pulse stopped, and the corruption began: trees twist into unnatural shapes, animals flee or turn aggressive, and a creeping black fungus spreads from the forest's core.

## Premise
You are Eira Mosswalker, a Ranger who has patrolled the Thornwood's outer trails for three years. Your animal companion, a young hawk named Cinder, spotted the corruption from above and led you to the forest's edge where you found the first blighted trees. The local herbalist, Tova Greenmantle, confirms the corruption is spreading toward the village of Roothollow and will reach it within days. She asks you to travel to the Hearthstone, discover what silenced it, and restore its power before the blight consumes the forest.

## Pre-made Character
**Eira Mosswalker — Ranger (Level 1)**
- **Heritage**: Elf (attuned to natural environments)
- **Community**: Wanderborne (raised on forest trails)
- **Background**: Thornwood patrol ranger, skilled tracker and herbalist
- **Key Domain**: Sage — deep knowledge of flora, fauna, and natural cycles
- **Secondary Domain**: Bone — survival instincts, animal kinship
- **Evasion**: 12 | **Armor Score**: 2 (leather armor)
- **Hit Points**: 14 | **Stress**: 2
- **Hope**: 2 starting
- **Animal Companion**: Cinder (young hawk) — can scout ahead, harass enemies (disadvantage on one attack per round), and deliver small objects
- **Notable Equipment**: Longbow, hunting knife, leather armor, herbalism kit, 50 ft rope, waterskin, trail rations (3 days)

## NPCs

### Cinder (Animal Companion)
A copper-feathered young hawk with sharp eyes and a bold temperament. Cinder can scout from above (revealing terrain and enemy positions), dive-bomb enemies (impose disadvantage on one attack per round), and carry small items. Cinder is Eira's primary tactical advantage in solo play.

### Tova Greenmantle (Quest Giver)
An elderly herbalist who lives at the forest edge. Tova is knowledgeable about the Hearthstone's history and provides Eira with a Purification Poultice (single use — removes one corruption effect from a creature or small area) and a Blight Map showing the corruption's spread pattern.

### The Hollow Voice (Mystery Element)
A disembodied whisper that Eira begins hearing after entering the corrupted zone. It speaks in fragments — warnings, pleas, and riddles. It is the fading consciousness of a forest spirit bound to the Hearthstone, trying to guide Eira to the source of corruption.

### Riven (Antagonist — Corrupted Beast)
Once a great elk that served as guardian of the Hearthstone glade, Riven has been consumed by the blight. Now a twisted, fungus-covered monstrosity, Riven patrols the glade and attacks anything that approaches the Hearthstone. Riven can be purified rather than killed if Eira uses the right approach.

## Plot Line

### Act 1 — The Outer Thornwood (Exploration & Tracking)
Eira enters the forest following the blight trail marked on Tova's map. Key beats:

1. **Tova's Briefing**: Tova explains the Hearthstone's role, gives Eira the map and poultice, and warns that the corruption affects minds as well as bodies.
2. **The Blighted Trail**: Eira tracks the corruption inward. Environmental details: blackened bark, mushrooms that release spore clouds, silence where birdsong should be. A Survival check (difficulty 11) keeps her on the safe path; failure means a detour through a spore cloud (1d4 stress damage).
3. **The Wounded Deer**: Eira finds a deer tangled in corrupted vines, partially blighted. She can free it (Finesse check, difficulty 12) and use herbalism to slow the blight. If saved, the deer later appears at the Hearthstone glade as a distraction during the final encounter.
4. **Cinder's Warning**: The hawk returns from a scouting flight with a torn piece of corrupted bark — something large is moving through the deep forest ahead.

### Act 2 — The Deep Forest (Survival & Combat)
The corruption intensifies. Trees are warped, the air smells of decay, and aggressive creatures roam. Key beats:

1. **Spore Hollow**: A depression filled with blight fungus that releases toxic clouds when disturbed. Eira must navigate through using Agility checks (difficulty 12) or find an alternate route (Survival check, difficulty 13 to spot a game trail above the hollow).
2. **Blighted Wolves**: Two wolves corrupted by the blight attack from the fungal undergrowth. Use Daggerheart adversary: Wolf (tier 1), with the addition that their bites inflict a blight condition (target takes 1 additional damage at the start of their next turn unless cleared with an herbalism check).
3. **The Corrupted Shrine**: A small wayside shrine to the forest spirits, now overgrown with black fungus. Eira can cleanse it using her herbalism kit (Knowledge check, difficulty 13). Success reveals a vision from the Hollow Voice: the corruption began when a shard of dark crystal was driven into the Hearthstone by an unknown hand.
4. **Camp and Rest**: Eira can make camp in a sheltered hollow. Cinder keeps watch. A short rest restores HP and clears stress.

### Act 3 — The Hearthstone Glade (Climax & Resolution)
The Hearthstone stands at the center of a circular clearing, now choked with writhing fungal growth. The dark crystal shard is visible, embedded in the menhir's face. Riven patrols the glade. Key beats:

1. **Glade Approach**: Eira can observe from the tree line. Cinder scouts and reports Riven's patrol pattern (circles the stone every few minutes). The fungal growth is thickest near the stone — movement through it is difficult terrain.
2. **The Shard**: The dark crystal must be removed from the Hearthstone to stop the corruption. It is embedded deeply and requires a Strength check (difficulty 14) to extract. While Eira works on the shard, Riven attacks.
3. **Riven Confrontation**: The corrupted elk charges when Eira approaches the stone. This is the climactic encounter.

## Key Encounters

### Encounter 1: Blighted Wolves (2 Wolves)
- **Environment**: Dense forest, fungal undergrowth, dim light filtering through canopy
- **Adversaries**: 2× Wolf (Daggerheart adversary tier 1), with blight bite (1 extra damage next turn)
- **Tactics**: Wolves flank — one charges directly, one circles behind. They use pack tactics.
- **Solo Adjustment**: Cinder harasses one wolf (disadvantage on attacks). If Eira drops below half HP, one wolf breaks off to circle warily, giving her a round to reposition.

### Encounter 2: Riven, the Corrupted Elk
- **Environment**: Hearthstone glade, fungal difficult terrain, the menhir at center
- **Adversaries**: Riven — use **Dire Beast** (Daggerheart adversary tier 2) with the following modifications: Riven's attacks spread blight on hit (1d4 extra damage); Riven has a charge attack (moves double speed in a line, +3 damage on hit, but must spend next action recovering).
- **Tactics**: Riven charges on sight. After the first charge, Riven positions between Eira and the Hearthstone, making extracting the shard difficult while in combat. If Eira uses the Purification Poultice on Riven (requires a melee touch — Agility check difficulty 14), Riven is stunned for two rounds as the corruption recedes, giving Eira time to extract the shard.
- **Solo Adjustment**: If the deer from Act 1 was saved, it enters the glade and distracts Riven for one round (Riven attacks the deer instead). Cinder can deliver the poultice if Eira ties it to Cinder's talons (Finesse check difficulty 13).

## Final Challenge
**Victory Conditions:**
- Extract the dark crystal shard from the Hearthstone.
- Survive or neutralize Riven.

**Alternative Resolutions:**
- **Purification Path**: Using the Purification Poultice on Riven before extracting the shard avoids killing the guardian elk. Once the shard is removed, the Hearthstone's pulse resumes and Riven is fully restored to its natural form. This is the "best" ending.
- **Combat Path**: Eira can fight and kill Riven, then extract the shard. The Hearthstone restores, but the glade mourns the loss of its guardian. The Hollow Voice is grateful but sorrowful.
- **Stealth Path**: With a successful Agility check (difficulty 15) Eira can reach the stone while Riven is on the far side of its patrol, extract the shard quickly, and flee before Riven reaches her. Risky but avoids combat entirely.

## Rewards
- **Experience**: Milestone — completing the adventure advances Eira to Level 2.
- **Items**: Dark Crystal Shard (can be studied or traded to Tova for a Blight Ward amulet that grants +1 to saves against poison and corruption), Hearthstone Blessing (once per day, Eira can cast a minor nature cantrip — grow a small plant, calm a wild animal, or purify a cup of water).
- **Reputation**: Roothollow and the forest communities know Eira as the Ranger who saved the Thornwood. Tova offers ongoing mentorship in advanced herbalism.

## Session Conclusion
As Eira pulls the dark crystal free, green light erupts from the Hearthstone and pulses outward in waves. The blight retreats visibly — fungus withers, twisted bark straightens, and color returns to the canopy. Cinder circles overhead, crying out in triumph. If Riven was purified, the great elk stands beside the stone, restored and regal, and bows its head to Eira before resuming its ancient patrol. The forest breathes again.

## AI GM Guidance

### Tone
Atmospheric and reverent. The Thornwood should feel ancient and alive — a place with its own rhythms and memory. The corruption is viscerally wrong, an offense against the natural order. Combat should feel like defending something precious, not just fighting monsters.

### Pacing
- **Act 1** is exploratory and atmospheric. Let the player soak in the forest descriptions. The Hollow Voice fragments should be cryptic at first, becoming clearer as Eira gets closer to the Hearthstone.
- **Act 2** increases tension with combat and environmental hazards. The Corrupted Shrine vision is the key plot reveal — present it dramatically.
- **Act 3** is a focused climax. The glade should feel like a sacred place desecrated. Give the player time to observe and plan before Riven attacks.

### Difficulty Knobs
- If the player struggles with the wolves, have one flee after taking significant damage.
- Riven's charge attack can be telegraphed clearly ("The elk lowers its antlers and paws the ground") to give the player a round to dodge or prepare.
- The Purification Poultice application can be made easier (difficulty 12 instead of 14) if the player has earned the deer ally or been creative in approach.

### Solo Adjustments
- Cinder is the primary tactical companion. Use Cinder for scouting information, combat harassment, and item delivery — but keep Cinder vulnerable enough that the player protects the hawk.
- The Hollow Voice provides narrative guidance without solving puzzles directly. Use it to hint at the purification option for Riven.
- Herbalism checks throughout the adventure give the Ranger class identity meaningful mechanical expression in solo play.
